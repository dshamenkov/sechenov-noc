Natural Language Processing for Scalable Mental Health Monitoring and Peer Support: Insights from the Open Dialogue Digital Platform

Dmitry Shamenkov¹, Alexander Lebedev², Elen Pogosyan²
¹National School of Public Health, NOVA University Lisbon, Portugal
²Opendialogue.space, Lisbon, Portugal

Conference: 19th Portuguese Health Economics Conference (CNES), October 30-31, 2025, Faculdade de Economia, Universidade do Porto
Abstract #165

The global burden of mental health disorders continues to rise, placing unprecedented demands on healthcare systems and underscoring the need for innovative, scalable approaches to early detection and ongoing support. Traditional assessment tools—such as questionnaires and interviews—are often episodic, rely on self-report, and lack ecological validity, failing to capture the dynamic, context-dependent nuances of daily emotional experience. Advances in artificial intelligence (AI), particularly natural language processing (NLP), offer an unobtrusive, real-time window into authentic language use that may bridge these gaps.

Objectives
Illustrate a use-case framework for applying NLP to continuous self-reflection texts within a digital mental health platform. Explore the feasibility of automated, language-based monitoring to detect emerging distress signals. Present initial observations on user engagement, alert generation, and peer-support dynamics. Outline next steps for deepening analytic scope, extending multilingual capabilities, and conducting longitudinal evaluation.
Methods
We describe the Open Dialogue Space—a user-driven digital environment for goal setting, regular emotional self-reflection, and structured peer-support groups. Anonymized entries undergo sentiment analysis, semantic mapping, and topic modeling, then are mapped to standardized scales (e.g., PHQ-9, GAD-7). A tiered monitoring protocol flags deviations from individual baselines and emergent themes (e.g., crisis language, self-harm ideation), issuing alerts to peer moderators with optional professional escalation. All procedures adhere to ethical standards on privacy and informed consent. Our report combines a live demonstration of core functionality with accompanying quantitative metrics and analytics.
Results
Linguistic-Clinical Alignment: Extracted language markers (e.g., sentiment shifts, coherence changes) align meaningfully with self-reported mood states. Signal Detection: The monitoring system reliably surfaces emergent risk themes, with alert patterns mirroring user-disclosed distress episodes. User Engagement: Pilot deployment indicates that in-platform prompts and peer discussions strengthen reflective practice and perceived social connectedness. Scalability Profile: Operational metrics reflect efficient, low-touch scalability suitable for large-scale deployment.
Conclusions
This use-case report confirms that integrating AI-driven NLP into digital mental health platforms enables scalable screening, timely peer-based intervention, and adaptive goal-setting. The platform's integration of refined linguistic markers, multilingual support, and longitudinal monitoring provides a robust foundation for enhancing clinical practice and informing health policy in data-driven mental health care.
